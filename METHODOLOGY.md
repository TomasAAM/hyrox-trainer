# Training methodology & evidence base

This plan trains a **Hyrox athlete** who competes **Doubles** but prepares to the
**Women's Pro** station standards. She follows her **own separate running plan**,
so this plan prescribes **no standalone runs** — only Hyrox-specific strength,
station/functional conditioning, and race simulations. It leans on the deep
strength, concurrent-training, and endurance literature rather than the thin
Hyrox-specific research, uses heart-rate zones from Garmin (no lactate test, no
pace), is periodized toward the race, and is auto-regulated by recovery data
(which already reflects her running, since her runs are captured by Garmin).

Each claim below is graded for evidence quality. Where strong evidence
contradicts popular folklore, the folklore is dropped.

## Principles & sources

### 1. Train station work at competition load (specificity)
Station strength-endurance is prescribed at the **Women's Pro** division standard
(sled push 152 kg, sled pull 103 kg, farmers 2×24 kg, sandbag lunge 20 kg, wall
ball 6 kg to 2.7 m, 100 reps; ski/row 1000 m; burpee broad jumps 80 m) so the
neuromuscular and metabolic demand matches race day. The evidence backs the
*method* (race-specific loading), not a universal number; exact kilos are
auto-regulated by her feedback.
- Reference — official HYROX station weights by division:
  https://www.hycrew.com/hyrox/weights

### 2. Heavy & explosive strength for economy and station power
Heavy or explosive low-rep strength improves movement economy and power output
with minimal added mass — directly useful for the sled and for efficient running
between stations. With full-gym access we program barbell and plyometric work,
not just station circuits.
- **Strong** — Llanos-Lagos et al. 2024, meta-analysis, endurance/running economy
  (Sports Medicine). https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38165636/

### 3. Concurrent training is compatible — interference is narrow
Lifting and endurance work together do **not** meaningfully blunt maximal
strength or hypertrophy. The one real interference is on **explosive power, mainly
in the same session / adjacent hard sessions** — so we keep explosive/plyometric
work **off her hard run days** (read from her Garmin-scheduled runs), but
otherwise train both freely.
- **Strong** — Concurrent training & hypertrophy 2022, systematic review +
  meta-analysis. https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9474354/

### 4. Mostly-easy aerobic base, sparing hard work
A large easy fraction with a small, potent dose of hard conditioning builds the
aerobic engine Hyrox demands while protecting recovery. Because she also runs on
her own plan, the Hyrox hard days are kept few and well separated.
- **Strong** — Rosenblat et al. 2019, systematic review + meta-analysis of RCTs.
  https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29863593/

### 5. Gradual load progression (not a rigid ratio)
Build chronic fitness gradually and avoid abrupt load spikes — measured on
**total** Garmin load, which includes her runs. We use this as a heuristic, not a
law; the popular ACWR "sweet spot" is statistically contested.
- **Contested** — Impellizzeri et al. 2020, conceptual critique of the ACWR (IJSPP).
  https://journals.humankinetics.com/view/journals/ijspp/15/6/article-p907.xml

### 6. Taper into the race
Cut volume ~40–60% over the final ~2 weeks while keeping race-load station
intensity, to arrive fresh.
- **Strong** — Bosquet et al. 2007, meta-analysis (*Med Sci Sports Exerc*
  39(8):1358–65). https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17762369/

### 7. Hyrox-specific demands (treat as directional)
Hyrox rewards aerobic + anaerobic power, local muscular endurance, max strength,
and economy *under fatigue* — hence the compromised-conditioning circuits and
race simulations.
- **Emerging** — Acute physiological responses & determinants in Hyrox 2025
  (Frontiers in Physiology). https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11994925/ —
  small literature; directional, not settled.

## How the generator uses this
The weekly generator references these **principles** (never inventing citations)
to produce a per-session `why` (justification + the trade-off) and a week-level
`methodology` note. The sources above are curated and rendered as a static panel,
so a citation can never be hallucinated.
